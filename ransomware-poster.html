RANSOMWARE KILL CHAIN

Not one event but a multi-stage intrusion that ends in encryption + extortion (the earlier you break the chain, the less it costs you)
CTF Farooq Ali
· security explainer series
v2026.06
01
The key idea — ransomware is a process, not a moment

Encryption is the last act, not the first. Before your files lock, attackers have already broken in, spread across the network, stolen credentials, and copied your data out.

Each stage is a chance to detect and stop them. Break the chain early and there is nothing left to encrypt.

02
The stages — one intrusion, left to right · break it before the lock

Stages 1–7 can run for days or weeks before a single file locks — that window is where defenders win.

03
Initial access — how they get in
🎣
Phishing — a malicious link or attachment lands the first foothold.
🖥️
Exposed RDP / VPN — internet-facing remote access with weak or no MFA.
🐛
Exploited vulnerabilities — unpatched edge devices and servers.
🔑
Stolen / bought credentials — valid logins sold by access brokers.
04
Dwell & spread — the quiet part

Once inside, they don't rush. Over days or weeks they:

escalate privilege→ map the network→ move laterally→ grab domain admin

All of this happens before anything encrypts — and it is loud enough to catch.

05
Double & triple extortion — why backups alone no longer save you

Because the data is stolen before it is encrypted, restoring from backup fixes the outage — but the leak threat remains.

06
Encryption / impact — the visible blow
🔒
Mass-encrypt files — servers, shares and endpoints, often overnight.
🗑️
Wipe recovery — delete backups & shadow copies so you can't roll back.
📝
Drop the ransom note — payment demand, deadline, leak-site link.

By the time you see the note, every earlier round was already lost.

07

08
Backups = last line — not the only line

Backups get you running again — but only if they're:

offline / immutableout of attacker reachtested restores

And exfiltration still means a potential leak — a good backup answers downtime, not disclosure.

09
Break it early — shrink the blast radius

Every stage before encryption is a chance to catch it. Three controls do the most work:

segmentation· least privilege· detection

Contain the intruder to one segment and the "kill chain" never reaches the lock.

10
Watch out
💸
Paying doesn't guarantee recovery — decryptors fail, and it funds crime.
📰
The leak is often the real damage — stolen data can't be un-stolen.
🛒
Ransomware-as-a-Service — affiliate kits lowered the bar for attackers.
🐢
Recovery is slow — plan incident response & business continuity now.
11
The takeaway
Ransomware is a kill chain — stop it at initial access, detect lateral movement, protect identities, and keep immutable backups. Once files encrypt, the early rounds were already lost.
Block the entry→ Detect the spread→ Protect identities→ Immutable backups
