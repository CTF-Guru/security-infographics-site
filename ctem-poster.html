01
What CTEM is — a program, not a product

Coined by Gartner, Continuous Threat Exposure Management is an operating model — a repeating program that finds, prioritizes, and reduces the exposure attackers could use, across your whole environment.

Broader than vulnerability management — it's a way of running security, not a scanner you buy.

02
Why it exists

Classic vulnerability management drowns teams in CVSS scores — thousands of findings, no signal on which ones an attacker can reach in your setup.

CTEM asks a sharper question: what is exploitable and reachable here — right now?

03
Exposure ≠ just vulns

A CVE is only one kind of exposure. CTEM counts everything an attacker can turn into a foothold:

vulnerabilitiesmisconfigurationsover-privileged identitiesexposed assetsattack paths
04
The five stages — a continuous cycle, run & re-run

Scope → Discover → Prioritize → Validate → Mobilize — then loop. The middle three turn a raw asset list into a short, proven, business-ranked worklist.

05
Validate · the differentiator

This is what sets CTEM apart. Don't assume a finding is dangerous — prove it's reachable and exploitable:

BAS (breach & attack sim)pen testingattack-path analysis

Validation cuts the noise — it stops the backlog before it forms.

06
Mobilize · close the loop

Turn validated exposures into remediation that actually ships — with owners and an operating rhythm:

assign an owner→ remediate→ re-run cycle

A finding no one owns never gets fixed.

07
Continuous, not a scan

Your environment and the threats against it change daily — new assets, new access, new exploits. A once-a-quarter scan is stale before the report ships.

CTEM is a rhythm you keep running, not a point-in-time snapshot.

08
CTEM vs Vulnerability Management — same goal, different operating model
DimensionVulnerability ManagementCTEM
CadencePoint-in-time scansContinuous program
ScopeKnown CVEsVulns + misconfigs + identities + paths
RankingCVSS scoreExploitability + exposure + business impact
ProofAssumed from the scoreValidated — shown to be exploitable
FramingA patch listBusiness-scoped exposure reduction
09
What each stage delivers
🎯
Scope — the business-critical systems & attack surface that matter.
🔎
Discover — the assets and their exposures across the scope.
📊
Prioritize — the few that are exploitable, reachable & high-impact.
🧪
Validate → Mobilize — proven true, then owned and fixed.
10
Watch out
🛒
"Buying CTEM" — it's a program & operating model, not a single product.
🌊
Boiling the ocean — skip scoping and you drown. Start with what matters.
📋
Skipping validation — without it, CTEM is just another huge vuln backlog.
11
The takeaway
CTEM is a continuous loop — scope, discover, prioritize, validate, mobilize — that shrinks the exposure attackers can actually exploit, not just the CVE count.
