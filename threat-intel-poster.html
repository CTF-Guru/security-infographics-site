01
What it is — data becomes intelligence when it drives a decision

Threat intel is knowledge about adversaries and their behaviour, gathered and analysed so defenders can act — not a passive feed to store.

02
The four types — sliced by audience & time horizon

🏛️ Strategic

Trends & risk for leadership — the big picture, business language.

🎯 Operational

this group, this campaign

🛠️ Tactical

TTPs for the SOC & defenders — how attacks actually work.

techniques & behaviour

🔬 Technical

IOCs — hashes, IPs, domains to feed tooling right now.

machine-readable indicators

Higher = broader & longer-lived (people); lower = narrower & shorter-lived (machines).

03
The Pyramid of Pain — how much it hurts the attacker when you detect at each level

Hashes & IPs are disposable — an attacker changes them for free. Force detection up to TTPs and they must re-tool their whole operation.

04
IOCs vs TTPs
🧩
IOCs — indicators (hashes, IPs, domains). Cheap for the attacker to swap; go stale fast.
🎭
TTPs — tactics, techniques & procedures. Expensive to change; describe behaviour.

Durable detections target behaviour, not disposable strings.

The intelligence lifecycle — a loop, not a pipeline · feedback steers the next cycle

09
Watch out — the ways threat-intel programs waste effort
⏳
Stale IOC feeds — indicators age fast and drown you in noise & false positives.
🗄️
Intel without action — a feed nobody operationalises is just expensive trivia.
🌊
Volume over relevance — a firehose you can't act on is worse than a few facts you can.
🕵️
Attribution theatre — chasing "who" for its own sake, over defending against "how".
10
The takeaway
Threat intelligence turns "what's out there" into "what we should do" — favour durable TTPs over disposable IOCs, and keep it relevant, timely & actionable.
Know the threat→ Detect behaviour→ Act on it→ Feed back & refine
