DSPM vs DLP

Two data-security jobs, often confused — one finds and maps your sensitive data at rest, the other controls its movement at the exit (inventory vs enforcement)
CTF Farooq Ali

01
The one-line difference — know where it is vs stop it leaving

DSPM finds WHERE your sensitive data lives and WHO can reach it. DLP controls and blocks data movement. Inventory vs enforcement.

02
DSPM — Data Security Posture Management

Discovers sensitive data across cloud, SaaS & data stores, then:

🔎
Discover & classify — finds PII, secrets & regulated data, tags what it is.
🕸️
Map access — who and what can reach each store.
⚠️
Flag exposure — public buckets, over-shared files, data in the wrong place.

“Do we even know where our crown jewels are?”

03
DLP — Data Loss Prevention

Inspects data in motion / in use and blocks or alerts on risky movement:

📧 email🌐 web uploads🔌 USB💻 endpoints

The exit checkpoint — it watches the doors data leaves through and decides: allow · alert · block.

04
The question each one answers

🗺️ DSPM asks

“Where is it, and who can access it?” — a live inventory & exposure map of data at rest.

🚧 DLP asks

“Stop it from leaving.” — enforcement at the boundary as data moves.

DSPM knows the terrain · DLP guards the gates.

05
Why DSPM rose — you can't protect what you can't find
cloud + SaaS sprawl→ data scattered everywhere→ shadow & forgotten stores→ DSPM to find it all

Data spread across accounts, regions & SaaS apps means no one has the full map. You cannot DLP — or govern — what you can't see. DSPM feeds DLP better targets.

07
Better together
DSPM discovers→ classifies→ prioritizes→ DLP + access controls enforce

Enforce on the data that actually matters — the crown jewels DSPM surfaced. DSPM makes DLP smarter: sharper targeting, less noise.

10
Watch out
🙈
DLP without DSPM = blind — no map means false positives on the wrong data and missed shadow stores.
📣
Noise burns out teams — untargeted DLP floods alerts; DSPM prioritization cuts the noise.
🏷️
Classification is the hard part — getting “what is this data?” right is where both tools live or die.
🚫
DSPM isn't a blocker — it's posture: it finds & maps, it doesn't stop movement. You still need DLP + access controls.
11
The takeaway
DSPM is your data inventory & exposure map · DLP is the movement control — find and classify first, then enforce where it counts.
Discover→ Classify→ Map exposure→ Enforce at the exit
