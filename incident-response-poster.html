INCIDENT RESPONSE

The repeatable loop for handling a security incident calmly — prepare, detect, contain, recover, learn (NIST SP 800-61 · SANS PICERL — a process, not improvisation)
CTF Farooq Ali
· security explainer series
v2026.06
01
What it is

A repeatable process to handle a security incident calmly — prepare, detect, contain, recover, and learn — instead of improvising in the middle of a crisis.

The plan you write on a quiet day is what saves you on the worst one.

02
It's a loop, not a line

Two names for the same lifecycle — NIST groups containment / eradication / recovery; SANS spells them out as PICERL.

🏛️
NIST SP 800-61 — Prepare · Detect & Analyze · Contain/Eradicate/Recover · Post-Incident.
🧭
SANS PICERL — Prepare · Identify · Contain · Eradicate · Recover · Lessons Learned.
03
The lifecycle — an animated loop · the retro feeds straight back into prep

NIST groups containment · eradication · recovery into one phase; SANS names each. Either way the last step — lessons learned — loops back into preparation.

04
1 · Preparation — before anything happens

The phase that decides how the rest goes. Build it while calm:

playbooksdefined rolestooling + loggingtested backupstabletop drills

You can't stand up a response team mid-breach — you can only run the one you built.

05
2 · Detection & Analysis

Detect it, scope it, triage severity — the three questions that shape everything after:

what happened?→ how bad?→ what's affected?

A confirmed, scoped incident beats a pile of unread alerts.

06
3 · Containment — stop the bleeding
🔌
Isolate hosts — pull the compromised machine off the network.
🚫
Disable accounts — revoke the credentials and sessions in play.
📡
Block C2 — cut the attacker's command-and-control channel.

Short-term containment buys time; long-term containment holds while you eradicate.

07
4 · Eradication — remove the threat

Root the attacker out for good — not just the symptom you first saw:

malwarebackdoorspersistencecompromised creds

Contain first, then eradicate — wipe too early and you lose the evidence.

08
5 · Recovery — back to normal, carefully
restore→ verify clean→ monitor

Bring systems back, confirm they're actually clean, and watch closely for reinfection before you call it done.

Restoring a still-compromised backup just restarts the incident.

09
6 · Post-Incident — the blameless retro that closes the loop

Blameless — hunt the process gap, not the person. Closing the loop is what makes the next incident faster to handle.

10
Roles & communications

🎯 IR Lead

Runs the response & makes the calls — one clear decision-maker.

🔬 Analysts

Investigate, contain, eradicate — the hands on the keyboard.

⚖️ Legal · Comms · Execs

Notify & disclose — regulators, customers, the business.

Decide who to notify and when before the incident — breach-notification clocks start ticking fast.

11
Watch out
🌀
No plan — improvising under pressure means chaos. Prepare first.
💥
Eradicate before containing — you tip off the attacker and lose control.
🧾
Destroying evidence — preserve logs & images before you wipe.
⏭️
Skipping lessons learned — then you relive the same incident.
🎭
Never drilling — tabletop it before the real thing, not during.
12
The takeaway
Incident response is a loop, not a scramble — where preparation and the blameless retro make every future incident faster to handle.
Prepare→ Detect→ Contain→ Eradicate→ Recover→ Learn↺
