01
What it is

SaaS Security Posture Management — tooling that continuously checks your SaaS apps for risky configuration. It's the CSPM idea (cloud posture) pointed at SaaS: M365, Google Workspace, Salesforce, Slack, GitHub, and the rest of the estate.

Not the infrastructure — the app's own security settings.

02
The problem

Every SaaS app is its own admin console with hundreds of security settings — owned by different teams, changed by different admins.

🧩
Fragmented — no two consoles look alike.
👻
Invisible drift — misconfigurations pile up unseen.
03
What it finds — the recurring classes of SaaS misconfiguration
🛡️
Weak admin controls — too many admins, no session limits.
🔑
MFA missing or optional — not enforced for all users.
🔗
Over-broad sharing — public links, anyone-with-the-link.
🔌
Risky OAuth apps — third-party integrations with wide scopes.
💤
Dormant admins — privileged accounts nobody uses.
🌐
Weak external defaults — guest & cross-tenant sharing wide open.
04
Third-party OAuth integrations — the shadow SaaS supply chain

Users grant SaaS-to-SaaS apps broad scopes with one click — access that lives on after they leave. SSPM inventories every grant and flags the risky ones — your third-party supply chain, made visible.

05
One view of the estate

Identity + config across every SaaS app in a single dashboard — instead of logging into each admin console to check the same handful of settings.

M365· Workspace· Salesforce· Slack· GitHub
06
How it works
connect (API)→ inventory→ benchmark→ flag drift→ remediate

Read settings, users & integrations via each app's API, compare to best practice, surface what drifted. A continuous loop, not a one-time audit.

07
SSPM vs CASB — posture of the app vs control of the access · complementary
 SSPMCASB
WatchesThe SaaS config / postureAccess & data in transit
QuestionIs this app set up safely?Who is reaching this app, with what data?
HowAPI — reads settings & integrationsProxy / gateway in the data path
Acts onSettings, sharing, OAuth grantsSessions, uploads, blocked actions

SSPM hardens the app; CASB polices the traffic — most estates run both.

08
Where it fits — SaaS is its own posture domain

☁️ CSPM

Cloud infra posture — IaaS/PaaS config (AWS, Azure, GCP).

🗄️ DSPM

Data posture — where sensitive data lives & who can reach it.

🪪 ISPM

Identity posture — accounts, entitlements & privilege sprawl.

SSPM is the missing peer — posture for the SaaS apps you rent, not the infra you run.

09
Watch out
🌑
Shadow SaaS — apps IT never approved, invisible to the estate.
🔌
OAuth grants — the new supply chain; a token outlives the user.
⚙️
Misconfig, not breach — the usual cause of a SaaS incident.
📡
API coverage varies — what SSPM can see differs per app.

The takeaway
SSPM continuously hardens your SaaS settings, sharing, and third-party integrations — the posture layer for the apps you do not host.
Connect the apps→ Inventory config & grants→ Flag the drift→ Remediate continuously
